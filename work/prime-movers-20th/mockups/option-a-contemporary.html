Conduct28
01
Confidentiality
What is shared in the room stays in the room. Names, attributions, and specifics do not leave with you.
02
Generous attention
Listen before responding. Assume the speaker has spent more time on the question than you have.
03
Disagreement, not disrespect
Push hard on the idea, never on the person. Your strongest critique should land as a gift.
04
One mic at a time
No side conversations during plenaries. The room only works if everyone is in it.
05
Care for the convening
Phones away, devices down, presence on. Twenty years has bought us the right to be undistracted.

Option A — at a glance

Direction
Contemporary / progressive, brightened. Reads as forward-looking, current-movement, modern philanthropic publishing. Closest reference: Aperture, MIT Press, contemporary foundation reports — but in spring colors.
Type pairing
Inter Tight (display, heavy weights for impact) + Inter (body) + JetBrains Mono (eyebrows, folios, technical labels).
Palette
Centella violet + global. Pale lavender paper (--violet-light #F7EDFF), violet-dark ink (--violet-dark #1E1A28) — body text passes WCAG AAA at 15:1. Bright violet (--violet #C77DFF) and bright orange (--global #FF9500) used as fill colors only — never as small text on the paper. Reverse type inside bright fills uses violet-dark for AAA contrast (6.3–7.7:1).
Two-family rule
The composition holds to Centella's "no more than two color families" guidance: violet and global, full stop.
Signatures
Tight grid + hairline rules on bios. Mono numerals as structural device throughout. Bright orange welcome panel (was dark) and bright violet pocket page give the brochure two saturated "feature" moments. Headline accent words sit inside violet swatches — the cheery moment in an otherwise quiet headline. Bio thumbnails use a violet→orange duotone wash.
Boxes
Centella panel pattern adapted to a contemporary aesthetic: 8px radius, hard edges, no inner frame. Urgent items shift to bright orange fill (was ultramarine). Practical info uses a 2-column grid of boxes; full-width boxes flag the things that should not be missed.
Quotes
Sprinkled in three places — thought piece (right, deep-ink box with orange edge), bios (right, bright violet fill with dark text), conduct (left, deep-ink box). Quotes are an active design element here, not decoration.
What participants will feel
Now, not nostalgia — and not solemn. The 20-year mark as momentum and warmth, not monument. Closer to a current-issue magazine in spring colors than a commemorative book.
