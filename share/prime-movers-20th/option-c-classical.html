11Code of Conduct— five numbered boxed items
VII.
Code of Conduct
1.
Be present.
Phones away during sessions. This room is small and the conversations are real. Give your full attention to whoever is speaking.
2.
Confidentiality, by default.
What is said here stays here. No recordings, no social media posts about specific exchanges. Chatham House Rule applies to every session unless explicitly stated otherwise.
3.
Respect the space.
You are guests in a working community. Be considerate of staff, of other attendees, and of the building itself.
4.
Speak from experience.
No abstractions when a story will do. We are here to learn from each other, not to debate positions in the abstract.
5.
Care for one another.
If something is not working — a session, an interaction, a logistical issue — tell a member of the production team immediately. We will fix it.

Option C — at a glance

Direction
Classical / editorial, brightened. NYRB / university press / privately published monograph — but in spring blossom colors. The 20-year mark earned, not announced, and not solemn.
Type pairing
Cormorant Garamond (display + body, italic-forward) + Inter (small-caps labels in 0.22–0.28em tracking).
Palette
Women Waging Peace (2001) — punchier rendering. Sourced from the original Women Waging Peace program book — the global initiative of the Women and Public Policy Program at Harvard’s Kennedy School. Sunny cream paper (#FFFAEC), navy display ink (#1B3A52, the “women waging peace” headline color, kept for body and headlines) — body text passes WCAG AAA at 11.4:1. Three saturated brights pulled from the original cover bands: orange #FF7A1F, yellow-amber #FFC130, and lime green #B8DD2F (the cover’s top band) — used as fill colors only. Decorative rules and frames shift from cool navy to warm amber, dropping the cool weight and pushing the page toward sunshine. Accent text uses deepened orange (#A33D0E, 6.2:1), amber (#7A5000, 6.8:1), or lime (#5A7100, 5.3:1) for AA. A direct visual quotation of Hunt’s heritage program identity, keyed up.
Two-family rule
The composition holds to Centella's "no more than two color families" guidance: tech and global, full stop.
Distinctive moves
Ornamental double-frame on cover with a soft orange sun-wash radiating from the bottom. Roman numerals everywhere — for sections, lists, conduct items — in deepened tech-pink. "MMVI · MMXXVI" date pair. Italic ampersand in deepened global. Drop caps in italic deepened-pink. Dark divider pages with huge gold roman numeral. Box motif uses an "outset label" — the eyebrow sits cut into the top border of each box, like a fieldset legend.
Quote treatment
Centered, italic, with double-rule top + single-rule bottom. Italic open-quote mark in deepened pink above. Inter small-caps attribution beneath.
Bio treatment
Italic monogram fallback in pale pink paper-2 swatch with thin plum border. Inter small-caps for org line.
What participants will feel
Permanence, with warmth. The 20-year mark earned, not announced — closer to a privately published monograph in spring colors than a black-tie commemorative.
